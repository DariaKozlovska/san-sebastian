ABOUT

San Sebastian

Donostia, as the Basques call San Sebastián, is a coastal city and capital of the province of Gipuzkoa, it is one of the most visited cities in Spain and famous for its beaches and world-renowned gastronomy. San Sebastián is made up of several districts, but first-time visitors will spend most of their time in the old town. It is the historic center of the city, known for its labyrinth of churches, pintxo bars and buildings dating back to the 19th century.

HISTORY

of San Sebastian

Donostia - or San Sebastian - traces its history back to 1180, when Sancho the Strong (Sancho el Fuerte) founded the city in what is now the Old Quarter. The city’s unique geographic setting made it a perfect port and military stronghold, thus walls were built to surround the city and protect it from outside forces. Mount Urgull was converted into a fortress, equipped with artillery. San Sebastian witnessed numerous wars and battles, was repeatedly looted and destroyed. But despite this, the locals did not lose heart and resolved to rebuild the city. The city gained popularity when Queen Isabella II had been visited it since 1845. Her summer visits to the city were the as the start of what would become a resort town. The Spanish aristocracy followed suit and caused the city to flourish both economically and socially.

MYSTERY

of San Sebastian

The whole Basque country is covered with secrets and legends that captivate the hearts. San Sebastian is no exception. Not only nature, yet the streets of this small town in the north of the country contain a lot of undisclosed and unknown things. More and more often curiosities are found in the mountains, and marvelous conversations between locals are heard along the streets, which makes you hold your breath every time you walk around this city.
